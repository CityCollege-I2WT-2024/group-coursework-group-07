Discover the Culinary Treasures of Thessaloniki

Your ultimate guide to the best food, restaurants, markets, and local food experiences in the city.

Thessaloniki’s cuisine is shaped by its rich cultural history, blending Mediterranean, Ottoman, and Balkan influences. The city’s strategic location as a crossroads of civilizations has left a lasting impact on its food, creating a unique mix of flavors and culinary traditions that reflect its diverse heritage.

Must Try Food Tips For Tourists Top Places
<

    Must try food

  • Bougatsa (Μπουγάτσα)

    What it is: Bougatsa is a delicious pastry that originates from Thessaloniki. It can be filled with sweet custard, savory minced meat, cheese, or spinach. The sweet version, filled with custard and topped with powdered sugar, is especially popular for breakfast or as a snack.

    Where to try it:

    • Bougatsa Bantis: Famous for the sweet custard variety.
    • Bougatsa Xanthakis: Known for its sweet bougatsa and crispy texture.
    • Bougatsa Panos: Popular for both sweet and savory versions.
  • Souvlaki (Σουβλάκι)

    What it is: Souvlaki is one of Greece’s most iconic street foods. It consists of skewered and grilled meat served in pita bread with toppings like onions, tomatoes, and tzatziki. Similar to Gyro, but meat is cooked on a vertical rotisserie.

    Where to try it:

    • O Kostas: Legendary souvlaki shop in the city center.
    • Souvlaki Kavouras: Known for tender, flavorful meat.
    • Lefteris O Kostas: Famous for crispy pita and succulent meats.
  • Kavourmas (Καβουρμάς)

    What it is: Kavourmas is a traditional Greek pork sausage, often served as a cold cut or appetizer. It's made by slow-cooking pork in its own fat with garlic, herbs, and spices.

    Where to try it:

    • Modiano Market: Butchers selling fresh kavourmas.
    • Laiki Agora: Great place to try fresh kavourmas.
    • Trigonio Tavern: Serves kavourmas with rustic bread.
  • Saganaki (Σαγανάκι)

    What it is: Saganaki is a fried cheese appetizer served with lemon. Common cheeses used are Kasseri or Graviera, crispy on the outside and gooey on the inside.

    Where to try it:

    • Tou Vasileiou: A popular taverna for Greek dishes.
    • Kavouras Tavern: Known for savory saganaki.
  • Greek Coffee (Ελληνικός Καφές)

    What it is: Greek coffee is a strong, unfiltered coffee served in small cups with a glass of water. It's a staple of Greek culture.

    Where to try it:

    • Modiano Market: Historic place to enjoy Greek coffee.
    • La Doze Bar: Trendy café for Greek coffee.
