Learn the dining customs and etiquette to make your food experience even better!

Greek Language: While many people in Thessaloniki speak English, it’s appreciated if you try to use a few basic Greek phrases. Locals will usually respond with a smile when they hear you make an effort.

  • Hello: “Yia sou” (informal) / “Yia mas” (formal)
  • Thank you: “Efharisto”
  • Please: “Parakaló”
  • Goodbye: “Antio”

Tipping Etiquette

  • It is customary to leave a tip of around 5-10% in restaurants, although it is not mandatory. Some cafes and restaurants may round up the bill or include a service charge, but leaving a little extra is appreciated.

Table Etiquette

  • Bread: Bread is a staple of Greek meals, often served with olive oil, olives, or as part of a meze platter. Don’t ever cut bread with a knife; instead, break it with your hands, which is more traditional.
  • Sharing Meals: Greek meals are often served family-style, with dishes meant to be shared. It’s common for everyone at the table to help themselves to a variety of foods.
  • Drinks: It’s polite to offer a drink to someone before you sit down to eat. If you’re drinking wine, you’ll notice that Greeks often serve it in small amounts, and it’s customary to clink glasses and make a toast. A common toast is “Yamas,” meaning “to our health.”

Coffee Culture

  • Coffee: Greek coffee is a big part of the culture. It’s served in small cups, brewed with finely ground coffee beans that settle at the bottom. Don’t rush through your coffee — it’s a social activity, and it’s common to enjoy a leisurely cup with friends or family.
  • Café Etiquette: Greeks are known to spend long hours in cafés, especially in the afternoon. When you order coffee, it’s perfectly acceptable to sit and chat for a while. Don’t expect fast service — the focus is on relaxation, conversation, and enjoying the moment.

Tipping in Street Food

  • For street food, such as souvlaki or bougatsa, tipping is not as common, but rounding up the price or leaving small change is always appreciated by the vendors.

Opening Hours

Restaurants and Cafes

Monday to Thursday: 12:00 PM - 12:00 AM (midnight)

Friday and Saturday: 12:00 PM - 2:00 AM (next day)

Sunday: 12:00 PM - 12:00 AM

Supermarkets

Monday to Saturday: 8:00 AM - 9:00 PM

Sunday: Closed or limited hours (some larger supermarkets may open in the afternoon on Sundays, typically 10:00 AM - 5:00 PM).
